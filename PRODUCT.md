# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Fitness enthusiasts who train at home or in small gyms with limited equipment.
They open the app mid-routine, often on a phone, sometimes with a coach's
mindset (tracking everything) and sometimes just wanting a fast workout for the
20 minutes they have. They own their AI configuration (OpenRouter key) and value
privacy — nothing leaves their device except the generation request they trigger.

## Product Purpose

Generate a personalized workout in under 10 seconds from four inputs (muscle
groups, equipment, style, duration), let the user run it with a built-in timer,
log completion, and surface 30-day trends. Success = the user opens the app,
taps through the wizard, and is training within a minute — then comes back
because the analytics make their consistency visible.

## Brand Personality

Precise · kinetic · trustworthy. The voice of a good coach: direct, no fluff,
quietly motivating. Confidence comes from clarity and speed, not hype. It should
feel like a well-built tool, not a hype machine or a gamified toy.

## Anti-references

- Generic fitness-app slop: neon-lime / aggressive-orange on black, stock gym
  photography, hustle-culture copy, badge spam.
- Subscription-wall SaaS dashboards with hero-metric templates.
- Over-gamified consumer apps (confetti, streak guilt, mascot characters).

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is the feature.** Every screen removes a tap. The wizard never asks
   twice; preferences persist and pre-fill.
2. **Data earns the dark.** The interface is dense with the user's own numbers;
   restraint everywhere else so the data reads.
3. **Blueprint, not billboard.** Cool indigo precision with one charged accent.
   Energy comes from motion and typography, never decoration.
4. **On-device honesty.** The privacy story is real; the UI never implies a
   backend that isn't there.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1, large/UI text ≥3:1 in both themes. Full
keyboard operability, visible focus rings, respects `prefers-reduced-motion` and
`prefers-color-scheme`. Color is never the only signal (selection also uses
checkmarks/borders). Touch targets ≥44px.
